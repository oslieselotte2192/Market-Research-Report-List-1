<p><p>The buffered fiber optics market is categorized into indoor and outdoor fiber optics. Indoor fiber optics are designed for use within buildings, featuring protective jackets that support sharp bends and ease of installation. These fibers typically operate in controlled environments, ensuring optimal performance for applications like data centers and local area networks. Outdoor fiber optics, on the other hand, are engineered to withstand harsh environmental conditions, featuring robust protection against moisture, temperature fluctuations, and physical stress, making them ideal for long-distance telecommunications and outdoor networks.</p></p>
