<p><p>Cartilage degeneration primarily affects joint function and pain management, leading to several treatment options. Osteochondral transplant involves transferring healthy cartilage from a donor site to replace damaged areas. Chondroplasty focuses on smoothing and repairing damaged cartilage. Joint replacement completely replaces the affected joint with prosthetics. Meniscus transplants involve replacing damaged meniscus cartilage with donor tissue. Cell-based cartilage resurfacing utilizes stem cells to regenerate cartilage. Other treatments may include regenerative therapies and minimally invasive techniques, addressing various levels of degeneration.</p></p>
