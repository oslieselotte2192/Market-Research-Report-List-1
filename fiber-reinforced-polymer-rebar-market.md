<p><p>Fiber Reinforced Polymer (FRP) rebar is a composite material made from polymers reinforced with fibers, typically glass, carbon, or aramid. It offers a lightweight alternative to traditional steel rebar, with advantages such as corrosion resistance, high tensile strength, and non-magnetic properties. These characteristics make it particularly suitable for various applications in construction, including bridges, marine structures, and building foundations, where durability and resistance to harsh environmental conditions are critical.</p><p>The Fiber Reinforced Polymer Rebar Market is poised for significant growth, expected to expand at a CAGR of 5.7% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing investments in infrastructure development, particularly in coastal and chemically aggressive environments where traditional rebar faces limitations. Additionally, the rising awareness of sustainable construction materials is propelling the adoption of FRP rebar, as it contributes to lightweight structures and lowers transportation costs. </p><p>Emerging trends include the development of new composites that enhance performance characteristics and the integration of advanced manufacturing processes to improve production efficiency. Furthermore, the rising demand for smart materials in construction is likely to influence the market positively, as FRP rebar can be embedded with sensors for real-time monitoring of structural integrity.</p></p>
