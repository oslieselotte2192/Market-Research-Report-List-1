<p><p>The geotextile market is divided into synthetic and natural types. Synthetic geotextiles, made from polymers like polypropylene and polyethylene, offer durability and resistance to environmental factors, making them ideal for various construction and erosion control applications. In contrast, natural geotextiles, derived from materials such as jute, coir, and sisal, are biodegradable and environmentally friendly, often used in landscaping and eco-restoration projects. Both types serve essential roles in civil engineering, agriculture, and land management, catering to specific project needs.</p></p>
