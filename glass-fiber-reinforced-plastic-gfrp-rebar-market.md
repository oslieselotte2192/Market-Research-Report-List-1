<p><p>Glass Fiber Reinforced Plastic (GFRP) rebar is a composite material made from glass fibers embedded in a polymer matrix, offering a lightweight alternative to traditional steel rebar used in construction. Its corrosion resistance, high tensile strength, and non-conductive properties make it ideal for use in environments where steel is prone to deterioration. GFRP rebar is gaining traction in applications such as bridges, parking structures, and marine structures, particularly in regions with harsh climatic conditions.</p><p>The GFRP rebar market is poised for significant growth, driven by increasing demand for durable and sustainable construction materials.
