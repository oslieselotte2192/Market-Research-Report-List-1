<p><p>Liquid Penetrant Inspection (LPI) equipment is used for nondestructive testing (NDT) to detect surface defects in materials and components. This technique involves applying a liquid penetrant to the surface, allowing it to seep into any cracks or pores, and then using a developer to draw the penetrant out, indicating the presence of defects. LPI is widely utilized across various industries, including aerospace, automotive, and manufacturing, due to its effectiveness and low cost.</p><p>The Liquid Penetrant Inspection Equipment Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.9% during the forecast period. The rise in infrastructure development and stringent regulatory requirements also contribute to market expansion. Key trends include the adoption of environmentally friendly penetrants, the integration of artificial intelligence for better defect detection, and the growing use of portable NDT equipment for on-site inspections.
