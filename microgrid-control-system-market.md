<p><p>Microgrid control systems are categorized into three main market types: Grid-Connected, Off-Grid, and Hybrid. Grid-Connected systems operate alongside the main power grid, allowing for energy exchange and enhancing reliability. Off-Grid systems function independently, making them ideal for remote areas where grid access is limited. Hybrid systems combine both approaches, integrating renewable energy sources with the grid or local generation, providing flexibility and resilience. Each type addresses specific energy needs and enhances overall energy management and sustainability.</p></p>
