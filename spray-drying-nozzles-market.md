<p><p>The spray drying nozzles market features two primary designs: swirl chamber and slotted core. Swirl chamber nozzles utilize a spiral design to create a rotating airflow, enhancing droplet formation and improving atomization for better drying efficiency. In contrast, slotted core nozzles employ a series of slots to produce a fine mist, ideal for uniform particle size distribution. Both designs cater to various industries, including pharmaceuticals and food processing, optimizing drying processes to achieve specific product qualities.</p></p>
