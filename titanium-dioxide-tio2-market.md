<p><p>Titanium Dioxide (TiO2) is a widely used white pigment and is produced primarily through two processes: the sulfate process and the chloride process. The sulfate process uses sulfuric acid and is traditionally cost-effective, producing high-quality TiO2 suitable for various applications. Conversely, the chloride process employs titanium tetrachloride and is more environmentally friendly, resulting in purer products with enhanced pigmentation properties. Both methods cater to diverse market needs, influencing pricing and production capacities in the TiO2 industry.</p></p>
