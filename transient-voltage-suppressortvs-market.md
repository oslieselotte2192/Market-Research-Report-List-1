<p><p>Transient Voltage Suppressors (TVS) are crucial components designed to protect electronic circuits from voltage spikes caused by transient events. The TVS market is primarily segmented into unipolar and bipolar types. Unipolar TVS devices are designed to clamp positive voltage transients and are typically used in circuits where only positive voltage spikes are a concern. In contrast, bipolar TVS devices can handle both positive and negative transients, making them suitable for applications where voltage polarity can vary, such as in AC circuits.</p></p>
